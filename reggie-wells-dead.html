Reggie Wells’ Career and Accomplishments

Reggie Wells’ journey in the beauty industry began after he graduated from Baltimore City College and the Maryland Institute College of Art. Initially, he pursued a career as a city art teacher before taking a leap of faith and moving to New York City in the mid-1970s. There, he honed his skills by working at various makeup counters and eventually secured prestigious positions at Glamour, Harper’s Bazaar, and Essence.

Wells’ talent and artistry caught the attention of numerous celebrities, including Oprah Winfrey, Beyoncé, Aretha Franklin, Michelle Obama, Diahann Carroll, and Halle Berry. He became a trusted makeup artist for these A-list stars, earning him a reputation for his ability to create flawless looks that enhanced their natural beauty.

His collaboration with Oprah Winfrey spanned an impressive 30 years, during which he served as her full-time makeup artist. Wells’ exceptional work on The Oprah Winfrey Show earned him five Daytime Emmy Award nominations, with a well-deserved win in 1995. His expertise and attention to detail were evident in every aspect of his craft.

Impact on the Beauty Industry

Reggie Wells’ impact on the beauty industry extended far beyond his work with celebrities. He was a trailblazer in creating foundation and powder shades that catered specifically to the diverse range of skin tones among his clientele, particularly Black women. His dedication to inclusivity and representation set a new standard in the industry, inspiring other makeup artists to follow suit.

Wells also shared his expertise and passion with his local community. He generously provided makeovers for seniors living in retirement homes in Baltimore, ensuring that everyone had the opportunity to feel confident and beautiful. In his 2000 book, “Face Painting: African American Beauty Techniques From an Emmy Award-Winning Makeup Artist,” he shared his knowledge and techniques, empowering individuals to embrace their unique beauty.
