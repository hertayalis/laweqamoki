Brad Lawing: A Tribute to a Football Legend

In a tragic turn of events, Brad Lawing, a former South Carolina football assistant, passed away at the age of 65. This article pays tribute to Brad Lawing's remarkable career, his contributions to South Carolina football, and the legacy he leaves behind.

Remembering Brad Lawing

A Beloved Coach and Mentor

Described as a true "guy's guy," Brad Lawing had an exceptional ability to connect with his players. Contributions to South Carolina Football

During his tenure at South Carolina, Lawing served as a defensive line coach, specializing in edge rushers and outside linebackers. From 1989 to 1998 and again from 2006 to 2012, Lawing played a crucial role in developing talented athletes and shaping the Gamecocks' defensive strategy. He worked closely with notable players such as Corey Miller, Gerald Dixon, and Henry Taylor, all of whom went on to have successful careers in the NFL.

One of Lawing's most significant achievements was his involvement with recruiting and coaching Jadeveon Clowney, who later became the No. 1 overall pick in the 2014 NFL Draft. Under Lawing's guidance, South Carolina's defensive unit topped the Southeastern Conference in sacks in 2010 and 2012. The team consistently ranked in the top 15 nationally in total defense between 2008 and 2012, achieving back-to-back 11-win seasons in 2011 and 2012.
